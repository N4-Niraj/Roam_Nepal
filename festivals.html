Festivals of Nepal

Colour, devotion, and celebration woven into everyday life

Celebrate with Nepal

Experience the world's most vibrant cultural festivals up close

Tihar (Diwali)

The festival of lights lasting five days, celebrating the bond between siblings, deities, and even animals like cows and crows.

Oct – Nov Hindu

Dashain

Nepal's biggest festival — fifteen days of worship, kite flying, swings, and family reunions honouring the goddess Durga's victory over evil.

Sep – Oct Hindu

Indra Jatra

Kathmandu's spectacular eight-day street festival featuring masked dances, chariot processions, and the display of the living goddess Kumari.

Sep Newari

Buddha Jayanti

A sacred celebration of the birth, enlightenment, and passing of Lord Buddha, observed with lantern processions at Lumbini and Boudhanath.

May Buddhist
🎊

Holi

The joyful festival of colours where streets erupt in clouds of coloured powder, water balloons, and music heralding the arrival of spring.

Mar Hindu
🏔️

Losar

Tibetan & Sherpa New Year celebrated with vibrant monastery ceremonies, traditional dance, and offerings in Himalayan communities.

Feb – Mar Buddhist

📅 Festival Calendar

January

Maghe Sankranti — winter solstice festival with special foods and ritual bathing at river confluences.

February – March

Losar (Tibetan New Year) and Holi — colour festival celebrated nationwide.

April

Nepali New Year (Bisket Jatra) and Rato Machhindranath chariot festival begin in Bhaktapur and Patan.

May

Buddha Jayanti celebrated at Lumbini, Swayambhunath, and Boudhanath with thousands of pilgrims.

July – August

Janai Purnima — sacred thread festival, and Gai Jatra — a colourful procession honouring the recently deceased.

September

Indra Jatra — Kathmandu's grandest street festival with the chariot of Kumari, the living goddess.

October

Dashain — Nepal's most important festival with kite flying, swings, and family gatherings.

October – November

Tihar (Diwali) — five nights of lights, flower garlands, and the beautiful Bhai Tika sibling ceremony.
