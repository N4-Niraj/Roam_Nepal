Wildlife of Nepal

A journey through the diverse fauna of the Himalayan region

Explore Himalayan Wildlife

Nepal’s wildlife is as diverse as its landscapes, ranging from the dense jungles of the Terai to the icy heights of the Himalayas. Home to rare and iconic species, the country offers a unique glimpse into ecosystems shaped by altitude, climate, and centuries of natural evolution.

From the elusive snow leopard prowling high mountain ridges to the one-horned rhinoceros grazing in subtropical grasslands, Nepal’s fauna reflects a remarkable balance between wilderness and conservation. National parks and reserves protect these habitats, allowing visitors to experience nature in its most raw and beautiful form.

Bengal Tiger

A powerful and elusive predator found in Nepal’s southern jungles, especially in protected reserves.

One-horned Rhinoceros

This rare species thrives in Nepal’s grasslands and wetlands, known for its single horn and armor-like skin.

Asian Elephant

The largest land mammal in Asia, found in Nepal’s subtropical forests and grasslands.

Himalayan Monal

The national bird of Nepal, known for its vibrant plumage and found in the alpine forests of the Himalayas.

Red Panda

A cute and elusive mammal found in the temperate forests of the Himalayas, known for its distinctive reddish-brown fur.

Snow Leopard

A mysterious and endangered big cat found in the high mountains of the Himalayas, known for its thick fur and incredible agility.
